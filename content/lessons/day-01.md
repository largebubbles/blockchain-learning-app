# What is Money?

## Learning Objective
Understand money as a tool for exchange

## The Story of Trade

Imagine you're a farmer with 100 apples. You want a pair of shoes, but the shoemaker doesn't want applesâ€”they want rice. Now you have to find someone who has rice and wants apples, then trade the rice for shoes. This is exhausting!

This problem is called the **"double coincidence of wants"**â€”you need to find someone who has what you want AND wants what you have.

## Money Solves This

Money is like a universal gift card that everyone accepts. Instead of trading apples for rice for shoes, you can:
1. Sell your apples for money
2. Use that money to buy shoes directly

Simple!

## What Makes Something "Money"?

Money does three important jobs:

1. **Store of Value** - You can save it for later
2. **Medium of Exchange** - Everyone accepts it for trade
3. **Unit of Account** - You can measure the value of things (this costs $10, that costs $50)

## Key Takeaways

- Money solves the problem of finding someone to trade with
- It stores value, enables exchange, and measures worth
- Money has evolved from physical objects to digital numbers
- The best money is something everyone trusts and accepts

## Think About It

Most "money" today is just numbers in a computer. When you check your bank balance, you're looking at digits on a screen. If money can be digital, what else could it become?
